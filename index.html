## CommonsPub the quest for a modular decentralised app ecosystem
#### goal Empower people to create and self-manage social spaces and tools tailored to their needs.
#### Main downsides of social platforms 1. Centralised (monopolistic network effects) 1. Proprietary (black box) 1. Surveillance (you are the product) 1. Attention optimisation algorithms 1. Capitalist (sustainability is not enough)
#### Additional downsides of social platforms 1. Squeeze complex social dynamics into simplistic products 1. Top-down product design 1. Filter bubbles 1. Different activities require signing up to multiple different services 1. Services shutting down 1. Developers making decisions instead of users 1. One-size-fits-all 1. Identity and social graph tied to 3rd party service

ActivityPub and the fediverse today are great for data-level interoperability, between different apps on different servers CommonsPub wants software-level interoperability as well, with different apps sharing large parts of their code
Instead of a top-down “product design” approach to building an app or platform, we’d like a world where even non-technical users and communities can do it themselves
You should able to piece together custom experiences from an ecosystem of apps and plugins developed by different groups who all contribute to (and benefit from) a pool of interdependent software libraries, while each focusing on different use cases, features and user experiences.
We have implemented groups (to further decentralise the fediverse by rendering users and communities independent from the instance they’re on),
Well we're really trying to be *incredibly* reusable. We want people to be able to reuse our models and add their own fields after the fact etc., none of which are supported by our database library.
We're trying to build an ecosystem where you can build apps easily - just pull in a few libraries, add some configuration, copy and tweak some queries and boom - done. that is a kind of magic, i suppose, so of course it takes magic.
but what none of this really tells you is how much fun it is to build! when you suddenly have extreme freedom to go from thought to execution (that execution time doesn't lag behind thought time too much), you just find yourself building cool things instead of puzzling them out!
it's like a framework, except it's just a bunch of libraries and that build upon existing frameworks and librarie (like Phoenix for web and Ector for database), as well as sample apps which you can use/modify/extend/fork...
* an ActivityPub library that is de-coupled from the rest of the app * a universal database identifier keying scheme * config-driven database schemas + validation * a lot of convenience apis around the database
We have decoupled the federation and storage of ActivityStreams activities/objects from the app’s relational database, backend logic, APIs and frontend interfaces.
package up models that we can share with other applications that have a slightly different view of the world.
Users can have one fediverse identity and a timeline which shows all types of content and activities in one place, while participating in multiple communities and using specialised apps for different activities.
Federated taxonomies for opt-in search indexing, and topic-based discovery across instances.

# Bonfire
#### 1. Bonfire is a device #### 2. Bonfire is a fediverse hosting service #### 3. Bonfire is a CommonsPub app
### 1. Bonfire is a device - use CommonsPub apps offline or with people on your local network (Temporary Autonomous Zones) - ready to use (plug in and play) - based on Rasperry Pi - runs CommonsPub apps - install and remove any CommonsPub apps/extensions easily, no coding required - comes with all the building blocks for creating your own apps
### 2. Bonfire is a fediverse hosting service - syncs between your Bonfire and the fediverse whenever your device is online - link it to your own domain name (or use `your-instance.bonfire.cafe`) - runs open source software, so you can self-host instead - open to cooperating with others wanting to run compatible services
### 3. Bonfire is a CommonsPub app - shows all your fediverse feeds and notifications in a single place - you can run it on the Bonfire device, or on your own computer or server - install and remove any CommonsPub extensions easily, no coding required - allows you to easily configure and customise everything
